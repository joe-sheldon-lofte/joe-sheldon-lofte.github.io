A thriving neighborhood shouldn't sound like a diesel engine idling at 6 AM. Earth Works is about restoring the "Silent City" by performing elite landscape and land management without the noise or the carbon footprint.

Ecological Services

Precision lawn care, advanced arboriculture, and drone-led property inspections powered by a 100% electric fleet.

Silent Lawn Care

You should be able to enjoy your yard while it is being maintained. Using 100% electric commercial-grade mowers and trimmers, we deliver precision landscaping at the earliest allowable hours without disrupting the peace of the neighborhood.

Data-Driven Landscaping

Landscape design should be an art form, not a noisy construction zone. By utilizing our advanced drone LiDAR data, we pinpoint your land's exact topographical needs in incredible detail. We prioritize "native-scaping" to reduce water usage, and we use only silent electric equipment for installation to keep your outdoor sanctuary perfectly peaceful.

Silent Arboriculture

Tree health is vital to the neighborhood canopy. Our professional arborists perform pruning, removal, and health assessments using battery-powered chainsaws and quiet chippers that deliver the same power as gas units without the ear-splitting noise.

Silent Snow Removal

Winter mornings are quiet, and snow removal should be too. We use high-torque electric blowers and plows. Our Silverado EVs effortlessly navigate icy residential streets with maximum stability and absolutely zero engine roar.

Water Control & Drainage

Climate resilience starts in the backyard. We provide emergency flood pumping and long-term drainage re-engineering, using high-volume electric pumps and precision grading to protect your home's foundation from water damage.

Advanced Drone Eco-Inspections

A bird's-eye view reveals the true health of your property. Equipped with RGB, thermal, and LiDAR sensors, our professional-grade drones create a complete hydrological and landscape picture. We map heat loss, tree canopy health, and drainage patterns to prove your property's ecological value to potential buyers.
